Throws are the **guard-breaking grab**. Because a throw **beats guard** and **cannot be [[vanish-z-counter|Vanished]] or [[super-counter|Super Countered]]**, it is the canonical answer to a turtling or counter-fishing opponent — the option that punishes pure defense.

## The numbers

- **Standard throw damage: ~1,250–1,312.** Modest on its own, but the *value* of a throw is that it **opens** a defensive opponent rather than the raw damage.
- **Ki-drain androids hit much harder:** [[android-19|Android 19]] and [[dr-gero|Dr. Gero]] deal **~3,594 / 3,875** on a throw — roughly **3×** a normal grab — because their throw **absorbs** the opponent's resources (the absorb-on-grab identity that also gives them their no-charge ki). See [[android-class|android class]].
- **Giants are throw-immune.** A [[giant-class|giant]] cannot be grabbed at all; instead it has a **stagger threshold** (4–5 hits to flinch). Throwing into a giant simply does nothing.

## Interactions

- Throws are the **counter to [[guard-and-chip|guard]]** and to the [[vanish-z-counter|Vanish]] / [[super-counter|Super Counter]] / [[perception|Perception]] defensive layer — a player fishing for those reactive options is vulnerable to a grab, since none of them stop a throw. This is the classic strike/throw mixup.
- They are **close-range** and committal, so good **spacing** beats them — and some specific throws are **escapable** (e.g. Cell 2nd Form's throw was made escapable in Dec 2024).
- Against the **ki-drain androids**, eating a throw is doubly bad: you take ~3,600 damage *and* feed their economy, which is why spacing out [[android-19|Android 19]]/[[dr-gero|Dr. Gero]] is a priority.
