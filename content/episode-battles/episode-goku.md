**Goku's campaign** is the **longest** in [[episode-battle\|Episode Battle]], covering **all 7 chapters** (Saiyan → Namek → Android/Cell → Majin Buu → Beerus/Frieza → Future Trunks → Universe Survival) and **available from the start**. Each new mission pays **15,000 Zeni** on first clear; this page tracks **28 main-line battles** plus three Sparking Episodes.

> **Saga gate:** completing **Goku's saga** unlocks the [[episode-future-trunks\|Future Trunks]] and [[episode-jiren\|Jiren]] campaigns. Nothing else in the mode opens them.

## The 300,000-Zeni farm

Replaying **"Super Saiyan Goku Awakens!"** (Namek arc) is the **best Zeni farm in the whole mode**: its first-clear reward **Goku (Z-Mid) Outfit 3** auto-converts to **300,000 Zeni** on **every subsequent replay** (research/06 §1.4). Set difficulty to whatever clears fastest — the reward does not scale with difficulty.

## Walkthrough & boss strategies

Most of Goku's 28 stages are routine on Normal. The numbers-first notes below cover only the fights that actually resist you, plus the time-limited what-if triggers.

### Stage 6 — Goku vs Great Ape Vegeta (the launch-era rage wall)

This is the single most community-infamous fight in the mode. It lands in **Chapter 1**, before you have learned the engine, and pits base/SSJ Goku against a **[[great-ape-vegeta\|Great Ape Vegeta]]** — a **[[giant-class\|Giant-class]]** body. Giants carry permanent **super armor**: your [[smash-and-rush\|rush and smash]] chains will **never make him flinch**, so the bread-and-butter "mash □ then △" combo that wins every earlier fight does nothing here except feed him a free counter-grab. Worse, his **grab is throw-unblockable** and removes roughly a full health bar, and at launch his tracking on it felt nearly homing.

The fight is won on **spacing and ki economy**, not combos:

- **Do NOT brawl up close.** His punches and grab are the kill threats. Stay at mid/long range and bait. Goku in this fight is *faster* than the Great Ape, so you can out-dash everything he throws — use [[z-burst-dash\|Z-Burst Dash]] (~0.75 ki, post-Jan-2025) to circle him.
- **Every Super/Ultimate he uses has a cut-scene tell.** When you see the brief pre-attack cut-in, **dash sideways** to dodge. Dodging is free; eating one of his beams is ~1 bar. There is no need to spend [[perception]] stocks here — the windows are huge and ki is your scarce resource.
- **Charge ki behind the rock columns.** The "Rocky Land" stage has rock formations. When you knock him down or after you dodge a super, **break line of sight behind a column and hold R2** (~1.4 ki/sec) to refill. This is the whole rhythm: dodge → break LoS → charge → punish.
- **Kamehameha is the win condition.** His beam-eating defense is poor; a clean **Super Kamehameha takes nearly a full life bar**, and the fight is **~4 Kamehamehas** end to end. Prioritise spending ki on Kamehameha over anything else.
- **Use [[sparking-mode\|Sparking Mode]] to create the opening.** Whenever you bank full ki + a skill stock, pop Sparking. In Sparking your actions are **ki-free** and you ignore ki-blast stun; mash Rush to land ~20 uninterrupted hits (super armor still won't flinch him, but Sparking rush chips and pins him), then fire a **boosted Kamehameha** (≈×1.5 the base value) before the gauge runs out.
- If it is still beating you, **lower the chapter difficulty at select**. Clearing on Easy unlocks everything (Yajirobe + the 60,000-Zeni Saiyan-Arc bonus) — there is no higher-difficulty gate on the reward.

### Stage 9 — Goku's Back, and the Spirit-Bomb what-if window

"Goku's Back" vs **Frieza (Z) Full Power** is two stages in one. The **default clear** requires the **Spirit Bomb**: you must grind Frieza's HP down to roughly **yellow** (~two minutes of fighting) before Goku unlocks the Spirit Bomb prompt, then **finish with it** to advance the canon story. The **what-if** ("A Hard-Earned Victory") demands the opposite — **defeat Frieza WITHOUT ever using the Spirit Bomb** (and without going SSJ in the scene). For the what-if, race his HP out with Kamehameha + rush-into-super before the Spirit-Bomb prompt becomes mandatory; if you trigger the Spirit Bomb you are locked onto the canon branch.

### Stage 3 — Rematch and Result (Pushing the Limit timer)

To branch into **Pushing the Limit**, **defeat [[raditz\|Raditz]] before the on-screen timer (~5:00) expires**. Raditz is weak; the only risk is dawdling. Open in Sparking if you have it (Goku starts with ki here), rush → △ → Super; he folds in well under a minute. The Sparking Episode itself ends on a **Goku vs Vegeta (Super Saiyan)** fight — treat that like any SSJ duel: win the **[[vanish-z-counter\|vanishing war]]** by keeping more ki than him (whoever has more ki wins the clash), and punish his recovery with a rush-super.

### Universe Survival closers (Stages 25, 27, 28)

The three [[jiren\|Jiren]] fights and **Anilaza** ([[anilaza\|Anilaza]], another Giant) are endurance checks, not skill walls — by Chapter 7 you have the full kit. For Anilaza, treat him like Great Ape: super armor, so space and beam him; his giant grab is the only real threat. For Jiren, charge to Sparking, land a short rush, **interrupt your own chain with a ki blast** so he can't escape-vanish a long string, then Ultimate on the knockback. Full Jiren tactics live on the [[episode-jiren\|Jiren campaign page]].

## Sparking episodes & 100% completion

Goku's saga has **three Sparking Episodes** (the long branching what-if routes) plus several minor alternate stages. Full 100% requires clearing all of them, each gated behind a separate trigger:

| Sparking Episode | Trigger | Length |
|---|---|---|
| **Side by Side** | In *The Earth's Mightiest Duo*, decline Piccolo / "Go it alone." | 13 stages |
| **Pushing the Limit** | In *Rematch and Result*, defeat Raditz before the time limit. (Also unlocks **Vegeta (GT) SS4**.) | 8 stages |
| **The Changing Future** | In *Hyperbolic Time Chamber*, choose "Go and help." | 4 stages |

**Minor alternate stages** (one-scene what-ifs):

| Stage | Trigger | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| *Arrival on Planet Namek* | Defeat [[jeice\|Jeice]] **without** deflecting his Crusher Ball | "Burter and Ginyu" |
| *Goku's Back* | Defeat Frieza **without** the Spirit Bomb | "A Hard-Earned Victory" |
| *The Cell Games Begin!* | **Defeat [[perfect-cell\|Cell]]** instead of letting the timer pass | "Homecoming" |
| *A New Power Awakens* | **Quickly defeat [[majin-buu\|Majin Buu]]** | "We'll Meet Again One Day…" |

**Missables to watch:** the branch triggers are mutually exclusive within a playthrough — e.g. you cannot get both the canon Spirit-Bomb result and "A Hard-Earned Victory" in one run, so plan to **replay** the relevant stage from the chapter-select to mop up the other side. Replays are cheap and still pay (and the Namek replay is the 300k farm).
