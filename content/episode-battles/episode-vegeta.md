**Vegeta's campaign** covers **chapters 2, 3 and 4** (Namek → Android/Cell → Majin Buu) and is **available from the start**. Each new mission pays **15,000 Zeni** on first clear; this page tracks **25 battles** including both Sparking Episodes.

## Walkthrough & boss strategies

Vegeta's main line is mostly even duels (Cui, Zarbon, Recoome, Frieza). The teeth are in the **Parental Bond** triple-Cell gauntlet and the **SSJ2/SSJ3 Goku** mirror fights, plus the timed branch conditions.

### The "defeat X quickly" branch triggers — what they actually demand

Three of Vegeta's branches gate on speed, and the engine measures them as **on-screen timers, not damage**:

- **Wind-Up Doll → Parental Bond:** beat **[[android-18\|Android 18]]** *fast*. 18 is a [[android-class\|Ki-blast android]] (Defense ~900, weaker vs melee), so she takes full damage from a rush string. Open in [[sparking-mode\|Sparking]] if you have it, land one short rush → △ → Big Bang Attack; she dies in a single Sparking rotation, well inside the window.
- **Super Vegeta → Total Victory:** beat **[[perfect-cell\|Cell]]** before Vegeta's "Final Flash" line (~2:00). Use Super Vegeta's high ATK: short combos into **Final Flash / Big Bang**, do **not** mash long chains — combo scaling is aggressive and a raw super out-damages a 30-hit string into the same super.
- **Vegeta's Determination → exclusive scene:** beat **[[majin-buu\|Majin Buu]]** quickly. Buu is a [[super-armor\|super-armor]] sponge with regen; race him with boosted supers in Sparking rather than grinding melee.

### Parental Bond — the triple Perfect-Cell gauntlet

This Sparking Episode is the campaign's endurance wall: you fight **[[perfect-cell\|Perfect Cell]] three separate times** (Power of Perfection → First Cell Games Opponent → Parental Bond), book-ended by a **[[super-trunks\|Super Trunks]]** fight. Cell is the problem child of PvE bosses — he **spams Super Kamehameha, interrupts your ki charge with ki blasts, and blocks/escapes long rush chains**. Beat him the same way each time:

- **Charge ki in the air, not on the ground.** Knock Cell up, and while he free-falls he can't shoot you — that's your safe **R2 charge** window into Sparking. On the ground he will snipe your charge with ki blasts.
- **Keep combos SHORT.** Cell escapes extended strings and the longer the rush, the more **[[skill-count\|skill stock]]** you hand him on rear hits, and the worse your damage scales. Use `□×4 → △ → Super` and reset.
- **[[perception\|Perception]] his beams when cornered.** Holding ○/B to Perception a Super Kamehameha costs **2 skill stock** but eats the whole beam and can flip into a Smash follow-up. Save it for when you can't dodge.
- **Super Vegeta's Final Flash is the finisher** — boosted in Sparking it clears a large chunk of a bar; land it on his recovery after a knockback. Budget two Sparking rotations per Cell.

### Number One Spot & the SSJ2 / SSJ3 Goku mirrors

The **Number One Spot** route ends on a **[[goku-z-end\|Goku]] (SSJ2)** duel; the main line's "The Long Awaited Battle" and "Saiyans: The Warrior Race" are **SSJ2 / SSJ3 Goku** fights. These are skill mirrors, won on defense:

- **Win the [[vanish-z-counter\|vanishing war]] by ki count.** Whoever has more ki when the clash resolves wins; if you're behind, **bail after 1–2 vanishes** (~0.5 ki each) rather than feeding a QTE you'll lose.
- **[[super-counter\|Super Counter]] (↑+□) is your free reversal** — ~2-frame window, **no resource cost**. Against AI Goku's predictable rush, counter the **launcher / second hit**, not the first. A failed attempt now eats a short lockout (Jan 2025), so don't spam it.
- **[[revenge-counter\|Revenge Counter]] (R3) for emergencies** — **2 skill stock** to break a combo from any direction when you have no other out.

## Sparking episodes & 100% completion

Vegeta has **two Sparking Episodes** and several minor alternate stages; 100% needs all of them.

| Sparking Episode | Trigger | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Parental Bond** | In *Wind-Up Doll*, defeat [[android-18\|Android 18]] quickly, then beat [[super-trunks\|Super Trunks]] at the next stage. | 4 stages; beat [[perfect-cell\|Perfect Cell]] **3×**. |
| **Number One Spot** | In *Vegeta's Wicked Heart*, **shake off Babidi's brainwashing**. (Pre-gated by finishing *Preparing for the World Tournament* at high health.) | 4 stages; unlocks **Goku (GT)**. |

**Minor alternate stages:**

| Stage | Trigger | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| *Super Vegeta* | Defeat Cell in a short span of time | "Total Victory" |
| *A Desperate Bid for Time* | **Defeat [[kid-buu\|Kid Buu]]** instead of stalling | "Saiyans: The Warrior Race" |

**Missables / notes:** the **Number One Spot** path is double-gated — you must *both* clear *Preparing for the World Tournament* without heavy health loss **and** pick "shake off brainwashing." If you took damage in the prep fight the option won't fully open, so replay it clean first. The Parental Bond and main-line Buu paths diverge from the same chapter; mop up the other side from chapter-select.

**Notable unlocks:** **Majin Vegeta** ("The Long Awaited Battle"), **Goku (GT)** ("Number One Spot"), **Cui** ("Frieza's Assassination Order").
